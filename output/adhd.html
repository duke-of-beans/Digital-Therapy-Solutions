Platforms We Recommend for ADHD

Each platform below was personally tested and clinically reviewed by Dr. Sarah Chen, LCSW — a licensed therapist specializing in ADHD and executive function disorders.

Cerebral

Best for combined therapy + medication management — the approach most ADHD adults need

4.6 Top Rated

Cerebral pairs licensed therapists with prescribing providers in a single platform — which matters for ADHD because most adults need both therapy and medication to manage symptoms effectively. Plans start at $85/month for medication management alone, or $365/month for the full therapy + meds combo. They accept insurance with an average copay of $30. The tradeoff: Cerebral prescribes non-stimulant medications only (Strattera, Wellbutrin, Qelbree) — not Adderall or Vyvanse. If your provider determines stimulants are the right fit, you'll need a different platform.

Done

Fastest path to ADHD medication — assessment to prescription in one visit

4.3 Recommended

Done is built for one thing: getting ADHD adults evaluated and medicated quickly. Your first appointment is a 30-minute video assessment, and if appropriate, you can leave with a prescription the same day — including stimulants. At $199/month after a $199 initial evaluation, it's not cheap, but the speed matters when you've been waiting months for an in-person psychiatrist. The tradeoff: Done is medication-first, not therapy-first. If you want CBT skills training or structured coaching alongside meds, you'll need to add that separately.

Talkiatry

Best for insurance-covered psychiatry — 60-minute appointments, not 15-minute med checks

4.4 Recommended

Talkiatry is psychiatry done right — 60-minute initial appointments with board-certified psychiatrists who can prescribe the full range of ADHD medications, including stimulants. They're in-network with over 60 insurance carriers, and most patients pay $30 or less per visit. The tradeoff: Talkiatry requires insurance (no self-pay option), and they focus on psychiatry rather than therapy. If you need talk therapy alongside medication, you'll need a separate provider for that piece.
