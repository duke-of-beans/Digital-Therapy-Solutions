Headway at a Glance

Headway

In-network access to private practice therapists — at your copay

4.4 Recommended
Our Verdict
Headway's model unlocks a tier of therapists that was previously unavailable to insured clients. The network is large, the directory experience is strong, and for insured clients it's one of the best ways to find a therapist you actually chose.

Here's the problem Headway is solving: most good private practice therapists don't accept insurance. The administrative burden of credentialing with insurers, submitting claims, and chasing reimbursements is genuinely prohibitive for a solo practitioner. So they go cash-only. And clients with insurance — who could be paying $20–50 a session — end up paying $150–250 out of pocket, or skipping therapy altogether.

Headway takes over the insurance side of the operation on behalf of those therapists. They handle credentialing, billing, and claims — the therapist just sees clients. The result, from a client perspective, is a growing directory of private practice therapists who now accept your insurance. You browse profiles, choose someone, book, and pay your copay. Headway handles the rest. It's a real structural improvement to how therapy access works, and the network has grown fast enough that it's now one of the largest in-network directories available.

Pricing & Plans

With most major insurance plans, Headway sessions cost your in-network mental health copay — typically $20–50 per session. There's no Headway subscription fee on top of that. Headway handles all insurance billing and claims on behalf of your therapist, which means no superbill submissions, no out-of-network paperwork, and no surprise bills. What you see at booking is what you pay.

Self-pay rates are available and set by individual therapists — usually $100–200/session. The same caveat applies here as with Grow Therapy: without insurance, you're paying private practice rates with no platform advantage. Headway's value is almost entirely for insured clients.

Deductibles matter here:

If you haven't hit your annual deductible, you'll pay more than your listed copay — sometimes the full session rate — until the deductible is met. This is true with any insurance-based platform. Check your deductible status before your first session so the cost isn't a surprise. Headway shows estimated costs during booking, which helps.

The bottleneck for in-network therapy has historically been credentialing — most private practice therapists can't absorb that administrative load alone. Headway removes it. What you get on the other side is a meaningfully larger pool of good therapists who now take your insurance.

Session Formats

Headway supports video and in-person sessions, with format availability determined by each individual therapist. Video sessions are conducted through Headway's integrated platform. In-person sessions happen at the therapist's practice location. Both are covered under your insurance through Headway's billing system.

Video sessions

Managed through Headway's built-in video system. Most therapists on the network offer video. The interface is straightforward — you join through the appointment link in your Headway account.

In-person sessions

Available from therapists with a physical practice location. For in-person, Headway still handles insurance billing — the therapist doesn't have to change their billing workflow. You pay the same copay you would for video.

Like Grow Therapy, Headway doesn't offer between-session messaging or platform-based tools. It's an appointment system, not a subscription service. Your relationship is with your therapist, not with the platform.

What We Like

Access to a tier of therapists that was previously locked
Many therapists on Headway are experienced private practice clinicians who weren't previously accessible to insured clients. They joined Headway specifically to expand their reach without taking on insurance administration. The quality ceiling here is genuinely higher than on platforms that primarily employ salaried therapists.
True insurance billing — no paperwork on your end
Headway files the claim, collects from the insurer, and charges you the remainder. You never submit a superbill. You never wait on reimbursement. The process is as clean as it gets for insurance-covered therapy.
Rich profiles with genuine specialization depth
Headway therapist profiles are detailed — therapeutic approaches, conditions treated, populations served, identity information, session format, availability. You're browsing with real information, not a marketing blurb. That's how you find a good fit instead of luck into one.
Network that keeps growing
Headway has been one of the fastest-growing platforms in the space. If availability in your area or for your insurance was thin before, it's worth checking again — the directory adds new therapists continuously.

Where It Falls Short

No platform features between sessions
Headway is a scheduling and billing layer, not a therapy platform in the product sense. There's no messaging, no worksheets, no journaling tools, no group sessions. If you want any of those between-session touchpoints, you need a different platform. BetterHelp offers messaging and group sessions; Online-Therapy.com offers CBT worksheets and structured programs.
Deductible surprises catch people off guard
Headway shows estimated costs, but if you haven't met your deductible, your first few sessions may cost significantly more than your copay. This is an insurance mechanic, not a Headway problem — but it's worth understanding before you book. Call your insurer and ask about your current deductible status first.
No psychiatry or medication management
Therapy only. If you need a prescriber for ADHD medication, antidepressants, or other mental health medication, Headway won't cover that. Talkspace and Cerebral both offer combined therapy and psychiatry.
Almost no value without insurance
Self-pay on Headway means paying private practice rates directly — $100–200/session with no platform discount or subscription structure. There is no scenario where Headway makes financial sense for someone paying out of pocket. Open Path Collective is purpose-built for affordable self-pay therapy.

Who Headway Is Best For

Insured clients who want therapist choice, not algorithm matching
The entire model is built around you choosing your own therapist. If being matched by an algorithm to someone you've never heard of feels wrong — and for a lot of people it does — Headway's directory approach respects that.
People who had a therapist they liked but couldn't afford
If you've worked with a private practice therapist before and had to stop because of cost — it's worth checking if they've joined Headway. A lot of experienced therapists have credentialed through the platform specifically to make themselves accessible to insured clients.
People seeking in-person therapy with insurance coverage
In-person therapy is covered the same as video through Headway. For people who do better in a physical office — some people genuinely do — Headway is one of the few ways to find in-network in-person therapists at scale.

Who Should Look Elsewhere

Uninsured or paying out of pocket
Without insurance, you're paying full private practice rates with no advantage. Open Path Collective ($30–80/session sliding scale) is purpose-built for this. BetterHelp ($65–100/week) includes messaging and group sessions for comparison.
People who want platform support between sessions
Headway doesn't exist between appointments. If you process through writing, need a place to track mood or journal, or want to message your therapist between sessions, this isn't the right home. BetterHelp or Talkspace offer that infrastructure.
People who need medication alongside therapy
Headway is therapy only. For medication management alongside talk therapy, Talkspace offers integrated psychiatry through insurance, and Cerebral offers combined medication and therapy management.

Our Verdict

Headway is solving a real structural problem — private practice therapists who were inaccessible to insured clients are now findable and bookable through a clean directory experience. If you have insurance and want to choose your own therapist from a large pool of experienced clinicians, Headway is among the best options available. Without insurance, it has nothing to offer. With insurance, it's excellent.
