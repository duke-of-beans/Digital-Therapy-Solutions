Platforms We Recommend for Anxiety

Each platform below was personally tested and clinically reviewed by Dr. Sarah Chen, LCSW — a licensed therapist specializing in anxiety disorders.

Online-Therapy.com

Best for structured CBT — the approach with the strongest evidence for anxiety

4.8 Top Rated

Online-Therapy.com is built around CBT — the approach with the strongest research base for anxiety. Every plan includes a structured 8-section program with worksheets, journaling tools, yoga, and live sessions. The tradeoff: they don't accept insurance, and the structured format may not suit everyone. If you want a clear roadmap rather than open-ended talk therapy, this is where to start.

BetterHelp

Best for matching speed — connect with a therapist the same day

4.5 Recommended

BetterHelp has the largest therapist network in online therapy — over 30,000 licensed professionals. For anxiety, that means specialists in social anxiety, health anxiety, panic disorder, and generalized anxiety are all available. Most people connect with a therapist within 24–48 hours. The tradeoff: you don't get much control over your initial match, and it's not the most affordable option without insurance.

Talkspace

Best for insurance coverage — most major plans accepted

4.3 Recommended

Talkspace accepts more insurance plans than any other online therapy platform — including Aetna, Cigna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, and UnitedHealthcare. If your insurance covers therapy, Talkspace is likely in-network. They also offer psychiatry alongside therapy, so if medication is part of your plan, you can manage everything in one place. The tradeoff: the messaging-based format can feel impersonal compared to video-first platforms.
