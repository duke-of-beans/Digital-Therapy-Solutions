3.9 Recommended

Mindful Care is a psychiatric group practice that has expanded into telehealth, currently serving patients in New York, New Jersey, Illinois, and Texas. The platform offers both therapy and psychiatric care (medication management) with strong insurance integration for most major plans in those states. Mindful Care's model is closer to a traditional practice than a technology-first platform — the care is clinically rigorous, but the technology experience is less polished than platforms built from the ground up as software products. For insured patients in its service states who want psychiatry with real insurance coverage, Mindful Care is a solid option.

Insurance-based Insurance: Most major plans in NY/NJ/IL/TX Video In-person

Pricing & Plans

Mindful Care operates on insurance billing for covered patients in its service states. Most major plans are accepted within NY, NJ, IL, and TX. Out-of-pocket costs are your standard in-network copay. Self-pay rates are available but not published centrally — contact them directly for current rates.

Regional specialization means Mindful Care can pursue real insurance contracts in the markets where they operate — the tradeoff of geographic limits for coverage depth is often worth it for insured patients.

Who Mindful Care Is Best For

Insured patients in NY, NJ, IL, or TX seeking psychiatric care and medication management
Those who want both therapy and psychiatry on a single insurance-covered platform
Patients who prefer a practice-style model over a software-first subscription

Who Should Look Elsewhere

If you're outside their service states — Mindful Care doesn't operate nationwide
If you want a consumer-grade technology experience with messaging and app features
If you need mental wellness tools or between-session support beyond standard appointments

Session Formats

Mindful Care offers both video telehealth and in-person appointments depending on location. The video platform functions as a standard telehealth encounter — scheduled appointments with your therapist or psychiatrist. In-person options are available at practice locations in service states.
