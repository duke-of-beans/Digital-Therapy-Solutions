How we research, test, and rate every platform

We have affiliate relationships. We also have standards. Here's exactly how both coexist.

How we research platforms

Start with hands-on testing: we sign up for every platform we review. We go through the intake questionnaire. We get matched. We verify pricing by going to the payment screen — not by trusting the marketing copy. When a platform says "$65/week," we verify what that actually means at checkout.

We check insurance coverage in two ways: the platform's own insurance tool, and direct verification with the insurance carriers. We document where they differ.

We look at cancellation policies, refund terms, and therapist switching policies — the things people never think to check until they need them.

How we choose what to recommend

We don't recommend the platform with the biggest affiliate payout. We recommend the platform that's best suited to the specific condition or need the page is about. BetterHelp's large network makes it our top anxiety pick for matching speed. Talkspace's insurance coverage makes it the right choice for someone with Aetna. Online-Therapy.com's CBT structure makes it right for someone who wants a clear protocol.

"Best for [condition]" recommendations can be different platforms — and usually are. Someone with ADHD has different needs than someone dealing with grief.

How we make money

We earn commissions through affiliate programs when readers sign up through our links. Current affiliate relationships include: BetterHelp (via Impact Radius), Talkspace (via FlexOffers), and Online-Therapy.com (direct affiliate program). Other platforms may be added as we review them.

These commissions don't cost readers anything extra. The platform prices are the same whether you arrive from our link or directly.

Our affiliate commissions do not influence our editorial ratings, rankings, or the content of our reviews. We have turned down platforms seeking favorable coverage in exchange for higher commission rates.

Our update schedule

Pricing changes constantly — BetterHelp and Talkspace adjust rates seasonally. We check pricing monthly. Full platform reviews are updated quarterly or whenever we detect a material change in service (new therapist vetting, policy changes, etc.). The "Last Updated" date on each review reflects when we last verified the information.

Our review process

Author researches and drafts the review → licensed clinical reviewer validates therapeutic accuracy → author incorporates clinical feedback → review is published with both author and reviewer credited. Crisis resource pages are reviewed by a licensed professional before every update.
