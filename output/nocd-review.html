NOCD is the most specialized OCD treatment platform available online, and it's not particularly close. Every therapist on the platform is trained in Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP) — the gold-standard, evidence-based treatment for OCD. The platform pairs clinical ERP therapy sessions with between-session support, a peer community of people managing OCD, and structured tools for practicing ERP techniques outside of sessions. Most major insurance plans are accepted, and a self-pay option exists for those without coverage. If you have OCD, NOCD is the clear recommendation — the combination of ERP expertise, insurance coverage, and between-session infrastructure is difficult to replicate through a general therapy platform.

What We Like

Every therapist is trained in ERP — not just familiar with it
ERP is the gold-standard treatment for OCD, and most generalist therapists aren't equipped to deliver it well. NOCD's entire network is trained specifically in ERP. That's the difference between a therapist who's heard of ERP and one who can actually guide you through an exposure hierarchy.
Between-session infrastructure built specifically for OCD
ERP requires practice between sessions — that's the whole mechanism. NOCD's app includes exposure tracking, response prevention tools, and a peer community of people managing OCD. You're not just waiting a week between sessions; you're practicing the treatment actively.
Insurance coverage — most major plans accepted
Specialized OCD treatment is expensive out of pocket. NOCD accepts most major insurance plans, which makes its $20–40 copay range genuinely accessible. The self-pay option ($280/month) is also more competitive than trying to find an ERP-trained therapist in private practice.
Peer community with shared lived experience
OCD is frequently misunderstood and mischaracterized. A community of people who actually have OCD — not people who use the word casually — changes the support environment. Knowing others understand the intrusive thoughts, the compulsions, the rituals without judgment is part of the therapeutic ecosystem NOCD has built.

Where It Falls Short

OCD only — not a general therapy platform
NOCD is designed for OCD. If you're managing anxiety, depression, ADHD, or any other condition alongside OCD, NOCD treats the OCD and you'll need a separate provider for anything else. That's appropriate clinical scope, but it means managing more than one relationship if your needs are broader.
ERP is hard — the platform doesn't make it easy, just accessible
Exposure and Response Prevention works by deliberately triggering OCD anxiety and sitting with it without performing compulsions. It's uncomfortable by design. NOCD delivers it well, but it's worth knowing that "best OCD treatment" doesn't mean "most comfortable OCD treatment." People who drop out often do so because ERP is harder than they expected.

Who NOCD Is Best For

Anyone with OCD who wants the actual evidence-based treatment
ERP is what the research supports for OCD. If you've been in therapy with a generalist and it hasn't moved the needle, the likely reason is that ERP wasn't being delivered. NOCD is the most accessible path to a therapist who can actually do that.
People who want structure between sessions, not just weekly appointments
ERP requires active practice between sessions. If that's something you'd actually use — exposure tracking, between-session messaging, the peer community — NOCD's model suits it well. If you'd prefer a minimal platform with just live sessions, you might find the infrastructure more than you need.
Insured and self-pay clients — the pricing works for both
Most major plans are accepted at $20–40 copay. Self-pay at $280/month is meaningfully more affordable than sourcing an ERP-trained therapist privately. Both access paths are real options.

Who Should Look Elsewhere

People who don't have OCD
NOCD is OCD-specific. For general anxiety, depression, or other conditions, a general therapy platform like BetterHelp or an insurance-based directory like Headway will serve you better.
People who need conditions beyond OCD treated on the same platform
NOCD treats OCD. If significant comorbidities need addressing — ADHD, major depression, PTSD — you'll be managing separate providers for those. That's often the right clinical model, but it's worth knowing before you start.

Session Formats

NOCD sessions are video-based. The between-session support is delivered through the platform's messaging tools and peer community. ERP practice tools are built into the app, allowing clients to track exposures and document progress between clinical sessions. The app-based exposure tracking is one of NOCD's strongest features — ERP only works if you're actually doing exposures between sessions, and having a structured place to log them makes that far more likely.
