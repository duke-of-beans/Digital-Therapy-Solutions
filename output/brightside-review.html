Brightside at a Glance

Brightside

Therapy and medication management for depression and anxiety

4.2 Recommended
Our Verdict
One of the few platforms that genuinely integrates therapy and medication without bouncing you between separate providers. Focused narrowly on depression and anxiety — which is exactly what makes it work.

Depression and anxiety are the two most common reasons people seek therapy, and they're also the two conditions where medication is most frequently part of treatment. Brightside is built around that reality. You can get therapy, medication management, or both — on the same platform, coordinated between the same clinical team. That coordination matters more than it sounds. When your therapist and your prescriber aren't talking to each other (which is most of the time with separate providers), important information falls through. Brightside eliminates that gap.

The tradeoff is scope. Brightside doesn't treat OCD, ADHD, eating disorders, or most conditions outside its depression-and-anxiety lane. If that's not what you're dealing with, this isn't your platform. If it is, it's worth looking at seriously.

From $95/mo (medication) or $299/mo (therapy) No insurance accepted Video Messaging

Pricing & Plans

Brightside uses a subscription model with three tiers: medication only, therapy only, or both combined. Medication management starts at $95/month and includes prescriber visits, ongoing monitoring, and medication (billed separately through your pharmacy). Therapy only is $299/month. The combined plan — therapy plus medication — is $349/month, which makes it the most cost-efficient if you need both tracks. Brightside does not accept insurance directly, though some users submit receipts for out-of-network reimbursement through their PPO plans.

The math on the combined plan:

If you were getting therapy separately ($65–100/week through BetterHelp) and medication separately ($150–250/session with a psychiatrist), you'd be paying $500–700/month before medication costs. Brightside's $349/month combined plan is meaningfully less — particularly if medication management requires multiple early follow-up appointments, which it usually does when starting or adjusting a prescription.

The gap between what your therapist knows and what your prescriber knows is where things go wrong. Most people manage two separate clinical relationships and hope the information flows between them. It usually doesn't.

Session Formats

Brightside conducts all sessions via video. There are no in-person options. Therapy sessions are scheduled with your assigned therapist. Prescriber visits are scheduled with your psychiatric provider. Messaging is available with both between sessions.

Therapy sessions

Video sessions with a licensed therapist. Brightside uses CBT-informed approaches for depression and anxiety specifically. Session frequency is typically weekly, with messaging access between appointments.

Prescriber visits

Video appointments with a psychiatric nurse practitioner or physician. Initial visits are longer for assessment; follow-ups are typically shorter. Medication is prescribed and monitored through the platform; filled at your preferred pharmacy.

What We Like

Therapy and medication under one roof
Most people who need both therapy and medication are managing two separate clinical relationships that don't communicate. Brightside's combined plan puts both on the same platform with a clinical team that coordinates. When your medication is adjusted, your therapist knows. That coordination has real impact on outcomes.
Narrow focus means actual expertise
Brightside treats depression and anxiety, and that's largely it. The clinical protocols, the symptom tracking tools, the prescriber expertise — all of it is calibrated for those two conditions. Generalism is a tradeoff; Brightside made the other choice.
Built-in symptom tracking between sessions
Brightside includes brief symptom check-ins between appointments — standardized assessments for depression and anxiety that your clinical team reviews. This gives your prescriber real data on how you're responding to medication adjustments rather than just your recall from a biweekly appointment.
Medication-only plan for people already in therapy
If you already have a therapist you like but need medication management, Brightside's $95/month medication-only plan gives you prescriber access without abandoning your existing therapeutic relationship. This is a genuinely useful configuration that most combined platforms don't support cleanly.

Where It Falls Short

No insurance accepted
At $349/month for the combined plan, Brightside is a significant out-of-pocket commitment. There's no direct insurance billing. Some PPO plans cover partial out-of-network reimbursement — worth checking — but you're paying upfront regardless. If insurance coverage matters, Talkspace offers therapy plus psychiatry with insurance.
Condition scope is limited by design
Brightside is depression and anxiety. If you're managing ADHD, OCD, bipolar, PTSD, eating disorders, or most other conditions, Brightside will refer you out. That's not a failure — it's appropriate clinical scope. But you need to know before you start the intake process.
Therapy plan has fewer sessions than some expect
The therapy plan includes a limited number of live sessions per month — typically one. Between-session support is messaging-based. For people who want weekly live sessions, Brightside's therapy tier may feel lighter than BetterHelp or Online-Therapy.com.

Who Brightside Is Best For

People with depression or anxiety who want medication evaluated
If you've been in therapy and haven't seen the progress you hoped for, medication is a clinically reasonable next step for depression and anxiety specifically. Brightside makes starting that conversation straightforward — no separate psychiatrist search, no separate billing relationship.
People already on medication who want integrated therapy
If you're managing a prescription through a primary care physician who isn't a mental health specialist, Brightside's clinical team can take over that management with actual psychiatric expertise alongside therapy.
People paying out of pocket who need both tracks and want one bill
Separate therapy and psychiatry out of pocket easily runs $500–700/month. The $349 combined plan is genuinely competitive for that use case — and the clinical coordination is better than two separate providers who've never spoken.

Who Should Look Elsewhere

Anyone dealing with conditions other than depression or anxiety
ADHD belongs at Klarity, Done, or ADHD Online. OCD belongs at NOCD. Bipolar, PTSD, eating disorders — these need a platform or provider with those specific specializations. Brightside will tell you this in intake, but better to know before.
People with insurance who want to use it
Talkspace accepts major insurance plans and offers both therapy and psychiatry. If your plan covers online mental health services, that's worth checking before paying $299–349/month out of pocket.

Our Verdict

If you're dealing with depression or anxiety and medication is something you want to explore — or are already managing — Brightside is one of the most clinically coherent options for getting both tracks of care in the same place. The narrow focus is a feature, not a limitation. The out-of-pocket cost is real, and insurance users should check Talkspace first. But for the right person, Brightside is doing something most platforms don't: treating the whole picture.
