BetterHelp is the largest online therapy platform in the world, with over 29,000 licensed therapists and more than 278 million sessions delivered. Their matching algorithm connects most users with a therapist within 24–48 hours, and the platform offers video, phone, live chat, and asynchronous messaging. The tradeoff that defines BetterHelp: you get unmatched speed and selection, but you're paying $65–100/week out of pocket — they don't accept insurance. For people who want to start fast and have the budget, it's hard to beat. For everyone else, keep reading.

Pricing & Plans

BetterHelp bills on a 4-week cycle, not monthly, which is worth knowing when you're comparing costs. The platform charges $65–100 per week depending on your location and plan tier, which works out to roughly $260–400 per billing cycle. Every plan includes the same core features — the price difference comes from regional pricing, not feature tiers.

What every plan includes:

One live session per week (30–45 minutes) via video, phone, or live chat. Unlimited asynchronous messaging with your therapist between sessions. Access to 4 group therapy sessions per month (called Groupinars). A digital journal for tracking thoughts and progress.

How it compares to in-person therapy:

In-person therapy averages $150–250 per session without insurance. At BetterHelp's rate, you're getting weekly sessions plus messaging for roughly the cost of one in-person session. That's the math that makes BetterHelp work — but only if you don't have insurance that would cover in-person or another platform.

Financial aid:

BetterHelp offers reduced-cost memberships for users who qualify based on financial need. During signup, there's an option to request financial assistance — this can bring the weekly cost down significantly. The exact discount varies, but users have reported reductions of 25–50% off the standard rate. If cost is a barrier, it's worth applying — the process is straightforward and doesn't require extensive documentation.

Session Formats

BetterHelp gives you more ways to communicate with your therapist than most platforms. This flexibility is one of its genuine strengths — you can switch between formats depending on what you need that week.

Video sessions

Face-to-face through the app or browser. 30–45 minutes per session. This is the closest to traditional therapy and where most of the deeper work happens.

Phone sessions

Audio-only calls for days when you don't want to be on camera. Same session length. Some users find they open up more without the visual component.

Live chat

Real-time text conversation with your therapist during a scheduled session. Useful if you process better through writing or can't talk aloud where you are.

Async messaging

Send messages anytime through your dedicated therapy room. Your therapist responds during business hours. Think of it as ongoing support between live sessions.

Insurance Coverage

If your insurance covers online therapy, you could be paying $15–30 per session instead of $65–100 per week. Check before you commit.

This is the biggest thing to know about BetterHelp: they do not accept insurance directly. There are no copay options, no in-network benefits, no direct billing to your carrier. You pay BetterHelp out of pocket, period.

That said, some users successfully submit BetterHelp receipts to their insurance for out-of-network reimbursement. Whether this works depends entirely on your specific plan — many PPO plans offer partial reimbursement for out-of-network mental health providers. BetterHelp provides session receipts (called superbills) that you can submit to your insurer. But this is you doing the work, not BetterHelp billing on your behalf.

If insurance coverage matters to you — and for most people it should — Talkspace is the platform to consider instead. They accept Aetna, BCBS, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, and more, with average copays of $15–30 per session. That changes the math dramatically. What We Like

The largest therapist network in online therapy
Over 29,000 licensed therapists — only about 20% of applicants accepted. In practice, this means you're more likely to find someone who specializes in exactly what you need, whether that's social anxiety, ADHD, grief, or something more niche. For breadth of expertise, nobody comes close.
Fast matching — often same-day
BetterHelp's algorithm matches most users with a therapist within 24–48 hours. When you've finally decided to get help, waiting 2–3 weeks for an in-person appointment can feel unbearable. BetterHelp eliminates that wait almost entirely.
Four session formats give you real flexibility
Video, phone, live chat, and messaging — all in one subscription. The ability to switch formats week to week means you're more likely to show up consistently. And consistency is what makes therapy work.
Financial aid available for qualifying users
BetterHelp offers reduced rates for users who demonstrate financial need — the application is built into signup and can bring costs down 25–50%. Worth applying before assuming you can't afford it.

Where It Falls Short

No insurance accepted — and it's not cheap
At $65–100/week, BetterHelp costs $260–400 per billing cycle with zero insurance offset. For someone with Aetna or BCBS, that same money could get multiple sessions at $15–30 each through Talkspace. Check if your insurance covers online therapy before committing to out-of-pocket.
You can't choose your initial therapist
BetterHelp's matching algorithm picks your therapist from your questionnaire answers. You can switch for free at any time, but you don't get to browse profiles before your first session. Platforms like Grow Therapy let you search and select upfront if that matters to you.
Therapist quality varies with a network this large
29,000+ therapists means inconsistency. Some users connect exceptionally on the first try; others cycle through three or four. BetterHelp's own data shows 86% would use the platform again — which means 14% wouldn't. More options, but more variability.
Subscription-only — you can't book a single session
BetterHelp requires a recurring 4-week subscription — no single-session trial. You can cancel anytime, but the minimum is one billing period. Online-Therapy.com offers a 20% first-month discount if you want lower initial risk.

Who Is BetterHelp Best For?

People without insurance who want the widest therapist selection
If you're paying out of pocket regardless, BetterHelp gives you the most options to find a specialist who fits your specific needs — 29,000+ therapists, only ~20% acceptance rate.
People who want to start quickly
Same-day matching means you can go from "I need help" to "I'm talking to someone" in hours, not weeks. If you've been putting this off and finally decided to act, BetterHelp removes the waiting barrier entirely.
People who prefer text-based communication
The messaging feature between sessions and live chat during sessions make BetterHelp strong for people who express themselves better in writing — or who can't always talk aloud where they are.
Mild to moderate conditions
BetterHelp works well for anxiety, depression, stress, relationship issues, and general life challenges. For more severe or specialized conditions, a platform with clinical specialization may serve you better.

Who Should Look Elsewhere?

People with insurance
If your plan covers online therapy, you could be paying $15–30 per session through Talkspace instead of $65–100/week at BetterHelp. Check our Aetna, BCBS, Cigna, and UnitedHealthcare guides to see what's covered.
People who need medication management
BetterHelp is therapy only — no psychiatry, no prescriptions. If you need medication alongside therapy (common for ADHD and some depression cases), Talkspace offers integrated psychiatry.
People with severe or specialized conditions
Conditions requiring intensive, specialized treatment — eating disorders, active psychosis, severe substance abuse — need platforms or providers equipped for that level of care. BetterHelp's general network may not have the depth required.
People who want to choose their specific therapist
If browsing profiles, reading detailed bios, and selecting your own therapist matters to you, BetterHelp's algorithm-first approach may feel frustrating. Grow Therapy and similar platforms offer a directory-style experience.

Our Verdict

BetterHelp earns its reputation through sheer scale and speed. If you don't have insurance, want the widest therapist selection, and value getting started fast — it's a strong choice. But if your insurance covers online therapy, or you need medication management, or you want a structured CBT program, other platforms serve you better. The right choice depends on your situation, not the biggest name.
