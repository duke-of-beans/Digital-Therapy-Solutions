Klarity at a Glance

Klarity

Fast online access to ADHD and anxiety medication management

4.0Recommended
Our Verdict
If you need ADHD medication and don't want to wait months for a psychiatrist appointment, Klarity solves that problem. Medication-focused, not a therapy platform — know the difference before you sign up.

Getting a psychiatry appointment for ADHD can take three to six months depending on where you live. Klarity's model is built around eliminating that wait. You complete an intake assessment, get matched with a board-certified prescriber, have a video appointment, and — if clinically appropriate — receive a prescription. Ongoing management and refills are handled through the platform. The focus is narrow: ADHD and anxiety medication management. There is no therapy component.

The important context: Klarity emerged during the post-pandemic ADHD medication boom, alongside regulatory scrutiny of telehealth stimulant prescribing. The DEA has tightened oversight of controlled substance prescriptions via telehealth. Klarity operates within those regulations, but it's worth knowing the landscape. For non-stimulant ADHD medication or anxiety medication (neither of which is controlled), this is less of a factor.

Pricing & Plans

Klarity charges a monthly subscription for medication management — starting around $99/month depending on the plan and condition. This covers prescriber visits, ongoing monitoring, and prescription management. Medication itself is filled at your pharmacy and billed separately. Klarity doesn't accept insurance, but FSA and HSA funds are typically eligible for the subscription cost. The initial evaluation visit is included in your first month's subscription.

The wait for an in-person psychiatry appointment averages 25 days nationally — but that's the average. In many areas, it's months. The population that Klarity serves is the one that simply can't wait that long.

What We Like

Fast access when local psychiatry is booked months out
For the person who's been managing undiagnosed or undertreated ADHD and can't get a local appointment for four months, Klarity's typical 1–2 week timeline to first prescriber visit is the entire value proposition. It's a real problem and this is a real solution.
Ongoing management, not just a one-time consult
Klarity handles the ongoing prescription management, refills, and follow-up visits that medication management actually requires. You're not getting a one-time prescription and being sent to your PCP — the clinical relationship continues through the platform.
Works alongside your existing therapist
If you already have a therapist you work with, Klarity handles the medication side without disrupting that relationship. Good therapy plus good prescriber management is a better outcome than either alone, and Klarity is designed to be the medication component of that pairing.

Where It Falls Short

No therapy component — medication only
Klarity is a prescriber platform. There's no therapy, no counseling, no behavioral support. For ADHD particularly, medication alone is rarely the complete treatment picture — behavioral strategies and therapy are part of comprehensive care. If you want integrated therapy and medication, Brightside (depression/anxiety) or Cerebral offer combined plans.
Stimulant prescribing faces ongoing regulatory scrutiny
DEA regulations around telehealth stimulant prescriptions have tightened since 2023. Klarity operates legally and compliantly, but stimulant prescriptions via telehealth require in-person follow-up in some states. Confirm Klarity's current prescribing policy in your state before starting.
No insurance accepted
$99+/month out of pocket adds up. FSA/HSA funds help but not everyone has access to them. If your insurance covers psychiatric services, it's worth checking whether Talkiatry (which accepts insurance and offers psychiatry) would cover this need at lower out-of-pocket cost.

Who Klarity Is Best For

Adults with diagnosed or suspected ADHD who can't get a local appointment
This is the core use case. Local psychiatry wait times can be prohibitive; Klarity removes that barrier for medication evaluation and ongoing management.
People who already have a therapist and just need a prescriber
If the therapy piece is covered and you need medication management layered in, Klarity integrates cleanly alongside an existing therapeutic relationship without requiring you to switch platforms.

Our Verdict

Klarity does a specific thing well: it gets you to a prescriber for ADHD or anxiety medication faster than local psychiatry typically allows. It is not a therapy platform, it doesn't accept insurance, and stimulant prescribing via telehealth exists in an evolving regulatory environment. Know those things going in, and for the right person — uninsured or insured-but-waiting — it's a legitimate, useful service.
