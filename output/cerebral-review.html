Cerebral had a difficult few years. Here's what that means for you in 2026.

Online therapy and medication management, with insurance. The platform restructured significantly after regulatory scrutiny in 2022. The current model is more limited — and more defensible.

Cerebral at a Glance

Cerebral

Online therapy and medication management — restructured, with insurance

3.7Use With Context
Our Verdict
A platform that overreached, got caught, and restructured. The current Cerebral is more limited than it was at its peak — that limitation is actually a feature now. For non-controlled medication management with insurance, it's functional. Go in informed.

In 2022, Cerebral became one of the highest-profile examples of what went wrong in the telehealth boom. The company faced a DEA investigation related to its prescribing practices for stimulant medications — specifically, concerns that ADHD diagnoses and stimulant prescriptions were being issued too readily, driven partly by growth incentives that weren't appropriate for clinical decision-making. The DEA inquiry was serious. Cerebral stopped prescribing stimulants in 2022. The company also settled an FTC complaint related to its cancellation practices. Its leadership changed. It lost a significant portion of its user base.

That's the history, stated plainly. What followed matters too: Cerebral restructured its clinical protocols, brought in new oversight, and refocused on non-controlled medications — antidepressants, anti-anxiety medications, mood stabilizers. The platform still offers combined therapy and medication management, accepts several major insurance plans, and operates at a lower price point than many competitors. The question isn't whether the history happened — it did — but whether the current platform is trustworthy for the narrower use case it now occupies. Our assessment: yes, for non-controlled medication management, with the full context in hand.

Pricing & Plans

Cerebral charges a monthly subscription for its services. Medication management alone starts around $99/month. A combined therapy and medication management plan runs approximately $325/month. Several insurance plans are accepted — Aetna, Cigna, and others — which can significantly reduce your out-of-pocket cost depending on your benefits. The medication cost itself is separate from the subscription and billed through your pharmacy. Confirm your specific carrier's coverage before signing up, as in-network status varies by plan and state.

Cerebral's narrower current scope is both a consequence of what happened and a reason to feel more comfortable with it for the right use case.

What Works Now

Insurance acceptance — a real cost advantage
For the right insurance plan, Cerebral's combined therapy and medication plan becomes significantly more affordable. Out-of-pocket combined therapy and psychiatry can run $500–700/month; insurance coverage changes that math considerably. Confirm your specific coverage before assuming it applies.
Therapy and medication in one place
The structural advantage of combined care still applies — your prescriber and your therapist are on the same platform with visibility into your overall treatment. For depression and anxiety specifically, where medication and therapy often work better together, having them coordinated rather than siloed has clinical value.
Restructured clinical protocols post-2022
The prescribing standards that caused the original problems have been revised. Cerebral now focuses on non-controlled medications, has tightened its clinical review processes, and operates under closer oversight. The post-restructure model is more conservative — which is appropriate.

Where It Falls Short

The regulatory history is real and worth knowing
The DEA investigation, the FTC settlement, the stimulant prescribing controversy — these happened, they were serious, and they're worth knowing about before you decide to use Cerebral. We're not suggesting the current platform has the same problems. We are suggesting you should be an informed consumer. Read about what happened, evaluate the post-restructure model yourself, and make the decision with full context.
No stimulant prescribing for ADHD
Cerebral stopped prescribing stimulants and has not fully restored that capability. If you have ADHD and need stimulant medication management, Cerebral is not the right platform. Klarity or Talkiatry are more appropriate options for that use case.
Better alternatives exist if your insurance covers them
If your insurance covers Talkspace (which offers both therapy and psychiatry in-network), or if you can access in-network psychiatry through Talkiatry alongside an in-network therapist through Headway, those combinations are preferable without the reputational baggage. Cerebral's value case is strongest where alternatives aren't accessible.
Cancellation experience has been a consistent user complaint
The FTC settlement was partly related to cancellation practices. Cerebral has since changed its cancellation process, but user reviews still surface friction here more often than with comparable platforms. Document your cancellation in writing if and when you decide to stop.

Who Cerebral Is Best For (Now)

People managing depression or anxiety who need both therapy and medication
The combined plan for non-controlled medication management — antidepressants, anti-anxiety medications — is Cerebral's defensible lane in 2026. For this specific use case with insurance that covers the platform, it functions reasonably.
Insured clients who've confirmed coverage and understand the history
If your insurance covers Cerebral and you're using it for depression or anxiety medication management — not ADHD stimulants — and you've made that decision with full context, it's a usable option. Informed use is the operative phrase.

Who Should Look Elsewhere

Anyone needing ADHD stimulant medication
Klarity or Talkiatry are better options for ADHD medication management. Cerebral no longer has a reliable stimulant prescribing pathway.
People who prefer not to use a platform with this history
That's a completely reasonable position. Brightside offers combined therapy and medication for depression and anxiety without the same history. Talkspace offers integrated psychiatry with insurance. Either is a clean alternative.

Our Verdict

Cerebral is not the platform it was in 2021, for better and for worse. The worst excesses of the telehealth boom — loose stimulant prescribing, growth-over-care incentives — got addressed through regulation and restructuring. What's left is a more limited platform: combined therapy and non-controlled medication management, with insurance accepted. If that's what you need, it works. If you need ADHD stimulant management, or you'd rather not use a platform with this history, there are clean alternatives. We've listed them. Go in informed either way.
