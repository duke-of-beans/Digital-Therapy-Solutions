We earn commissions. Here's exactly how it works.

This is the least fun page on the site. But you deserve to know.

What affiliate relationships mean

When you click a link on this site and sign up for an online therapy platform, we may earn a commission. This is called an affiliate relationship. The FTC requires us to disclose this, and we think you should know even if they didn't.

What this costs you: nothing. The price you pay for any platform is exactly the same whether you arrive through our link or directly through Google. The platforms pay us from their own marketing budget, not by marking up your subscription.

Which programs we participate in

BetterHelp: affiliate program via Impact Radius. We earn a commission on new paid subscriptions.

Talkspace: affiliate program via FlexOffers. We earn a commission on new paid subscriptions.

Online-Therapy.com: direct affiliate program. We earn a commission on new paid subscriptions.

Note: We review platforms we don't have affiliate relationships with. When we recommend a platform that doesn't appear in the list above, we receive no compensation from that recommendation.

How this does and doesn't affect our content

What it DOES affect: which platforms we've invested the most time reviewing. A platform with an affiliate relationship gets more thorough testing than one we haven't onboarded with yet. That's a natural reality we're being honest about.

What it DOESN'T affect: the scores, the "who should look elsewhere" sections, the tradeoffs we name, or whether a platform appears in a "best for" list. BetterHelp gets a 4.5/5 — not 5/5 — because its auto-matching removes control from the user. That's in the review. That's our actual assessment.

We've recommended platforms with no affiliate relationship when they're the right answer. We've written critical sections about platforms we do have relationships with. That's the policy, and it's non-negotiable.

How to tell which links are affiliate links

Most outbound links to therapy platforms are affiliate links. We don't add visual indicators to individual links because that creates visual clutter that's actually harder to parse. Instead, we disclose clearly at the top of every review and at the bottom of every content page: "Some links on this page are affiliate links."

Questions?

If you have questions about our affiliate relationships or want to know whether a specific link is an affiliate link, email us at contact@digitaltherapysolutions.com.
