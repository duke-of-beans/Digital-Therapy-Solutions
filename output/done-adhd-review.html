4.0 Recommended

Done ADHD does one thing: ADHD evaluation and medication management. The platform is designed to reduce the friction between suspecting you have ADHD and actually getting assessed and treated. The evaluation costs as a one-time fee; ongoing medication management is a monthly subscription. Done works with licensed psychiatric providers who specialize in ADHD. Insurance coverage is limited, but for people who've been on a six-month waitlist at a local psychiatrist, the accessibility gap Done closes is real. One important note: Done does prescribe stimulant medications, but the evaluation process is rigorous — this isn't a prescription mill.

Pricing & Plans

Done charges for the initial ADHD evaluation — a one-time fee that covers your assessment with a licensed provider. After diagnosis, ongoing medication management is /month. Insurance is not widely accepted, making this a primarily self-pay platform. Compared to a private psychiatrist (-500 for an evaluation), the math is favorable for self-pay patients.

initial evaluation

The six-month waitlist for an ADHD evaluation at a local psychiatrist is a real barrier. Done's platform addresses the access problem directly — not perfectly, but meaningfully.

Who Done ADHD Is Best For

Adults who suspect ADHD and want evaluation without a long waitlist
People seeking ongoing medication management after a diagnosis
Those who want a streamlined, ADHD-specific platform rather than a general psychiatrist

Who Should Look Elsewhere

If you want therapy alongside medication — Done is medication-focused only
If you need comprehensive insurance coverage
If you prefer in-person evaluation over telehealth assessment

Session Formats

The evaluation and medication management appointments are conducted via video. Between appointments, Done uses async communication for follow-up questions and refill management. The model is clinical rather than therapy-style — structured encounters with clear objectives.
