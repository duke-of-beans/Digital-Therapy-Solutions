    How Cigna Coverage Works

    Most Cigna plans cover online therapy. Your copay is typically $15–60 per session depending on your specific plan type. Before booking, check whether your plan has a mental health deductible — some do, while others exempt mental health from deductibles entirely.

    In-network vs out-of-network: In-network means the platform has negotiated rates with Cigna, so you pay a lower copay. Out-of-network platforms may still get partial reimbursement with Cigna PPO plans, typically 50-70% of the allowed amount.

    About MDLIVE: Cigna owns MDLIVE, which offers therapy sessions. However, the platforms reviewed above typically offer more therapist choice and specialization for mental health care.

    EAP coverage: Many Cigna employer plans include free Employee Assistance Program (EAP) sessions — usually 3-8 sessions per year. Ask your HR department if your plan includes EAP; it's often a hidden benefit.

    What If My Plan Isn't Accepted?

    Not every Cigna plan works with every platform. If you get a "not covered" result, you still have options:

    Out-of-network reimbursement
    Many Cigna OAP plans cover out-of-network mental health at 50–70% after deductible. Pay upfront, submit a claim for partial reimbursement. Call the number on the back of your card to check your out-of-network mental health benefits.
    Cigna EAP
    Even if your therapy coverage is limited, your employer's EAP through Cigna may offer free sessions — typically 3–8 — as a separate benefit that doesn't require meeting a deductible.
    Financial assistance programs
    BetterHelp offers financial aid that can reduce costs by 10–40%. Calmerry starts at $42/week without insurance. Both are affordable alternatives when insurance doesn't apply.
