Why this site exists

David Kirsch built Digital Therapy Solutions after spending weeks trying to figure out which online therapy platform was right for a family member. Every site he found either recommended everything ("all platforms are great!") or led with affiliate links without disclosing them. The pricing was vague. The "reviews" were barely distinguishable from the platforms' own marketing copy.

The mission became simple: build the site that actually does the work. Test the platforms. Verify the pricing. Check the insurance coverage. Talk to licensed professionals. Then write what we found — clearly, with tradeoffs named, with "who should look elsewhere" sections that other sites are too scared to include.

Our methodology

How we research and rate platforms: We sign up, go through the intake process, and verify what we find against the platform's published pricing. We cross-check insurance coverage by calling the insurance carriers directly. We test the matching algorithm. We note whether the "20% off" promotions are real or always-on tricks. We look for the gotchas in the cancellation policy.

Every platform review is then sent to a licensed mental health professional for clinical accuracy review. They verify that our descriptions of therapeutic modalities are accurate, that our "best for" recommendations make clinical sense, and that our "who should look elsewhere" sections are honest.

Our scoring rubric covers: pricing transparency, insurance accessibility, therapist quality signals, platform usability, format options, cancellation policy, and crisis response. No platform gets a perfect score — because none of them are perfect.

Our commitment

We earn affiliate commissions when you sign up through our links. We disclose this clearly on every page and in our full affiliate disclosure. These commissions pay for our research and keep the site free for readers. They do not influence our rankings — we've recommended platforms we don't have affiliate relationships with, and we've written critical reviews of platforms we do.

No platform has ever paid us for a favorable review. No platform has ever been told in advance what their score would be. If that ever changes, we'll close the site.
