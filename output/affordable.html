The Best Affordable Therapy Platforms

No insurance? These three platforms offer the lowest costs and best transparency for people paying out of pocket.

OP

Open Path Collective

Most affordable option — $30–80/session through a nonprofit collective

4.7 Top Rated

Open Path Collective is a nonprofit that connects you with licensed therapists at $30–80 per session — roughly 60-75% less than standard rates. There's a one-time $65 lifetime membership fee, then you book directly with therapists in their network at the reduced rate. Income eligibility: household income under $100,000. You choose your therapist from profiles and many offer video sessions. The tradeoff: reduced rates mean popular therapists fill up fast, and not all offer evening/weekend availability. But for the price, this is the strongest option for people without insurance.

BetterHelp

Best for financial aid — 10–40% off with their assistance program

4.5 Recommended

BetterHelp doesn't accept insurance directly, but their financial aid program can reduce costs by 10–40% — bringing the weekly rate down to as low as $48/week. You apply during signup with a brief questionnaire, and decisions come within 2–3 days. Financial aid lasts 3 months and can be renewed. With 30,000+ therapists, you'll find specialists for virtually any concern. The tradeoff: even with financial aid, BetterHelp costs more than Open Path. But you get unlimited messaging between sessions and the largest therapist network in online therapy.

Calmerry

Best budget subscription — straightforward pricing starting at $42/week

4.3 Recommended

Calmerry offers some of the most transparent pricing in online therapy — $42–70/week depending on your plan, with no insurance needed and no surprise charges. That's $168–280/month, which puts it between Open Path's per-session pricing and BetterHelp's rates. They include messaging therapy, scheduled video sessions, and CBT-based tools. The tradeoff: smaller therapist network than BetterHelp, and no psychiatry or medication management option.

What Affordable Therapy Actually Costs

The real numbers

Online therapy without insurance ranges from $30/session (Open Path) to $109/week (BetterHelp standard rate). Here's how to think about the cost.

Per-session vs. subscription

Open Path charges per session ($30–80). BetterHelp and Calmerry charge weekly/monthly subscriptions that include messaging + scheduled sessions.

Monthly math

Open Path at weekly sessions = $120–320/month. Calmerry = $168–280/month. BetterHelp with aid = $192–360/month. BetterHelp standard = $276–436/month.

Financial aid

BetterHelp (10–40% off), some platforms offer sliding scale on request.

Employer benefits

Some employers offer therapy stipends or wellness reimbursement even without insurance coverage. Check with HR.

HSA/FSA

If you have a Health Savings Account or Flexible Spending Account, online therapy is typically an eligible expense — even without traditional insurance.

Other Ways to Get Affordable Help

If you want more options beyond the main three, here are proven paths to affordable therapy.

Sliding scale directories

TherapyDen and Psychology Today both let you filter by "sliding scale" — many therapists offer $50–80/session rates for uninsured clients.

Community health centers

FQHCs offer therapy on a sliding scale based on income. Find one at findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov.

University training clinics

Graduate psychology programs offer therapy with supervised trainees at $10–30/session. Quality is often excellent — the trainees are closely supervised.

SAMHSA helpline

1-800-662-4357 for free referrals to local treatment and support.

Online-Therapy.com

$45–80/week with structured CBT program — another solid budget option.

Group therapy

Online group therapy (through platforms like Grouport) often costs $35–50/session and can be just as effective for conditions like anxiety and depression.
