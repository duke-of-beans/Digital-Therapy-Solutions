Open Path at a Glance

Open Path Collective

Sliding-scale therapy at $30–80/session for people who genuinely need it

4.1Recommended
Our Verdict
If cost is what's been keeping you from therapy, Open Path is the most legitimate solution that actually works. Real licensed therapists, genuinely reduced rates, no expiration on access.

Open Path Collective operates on a simple premise: therapists who want to serve clients who can't afford their standard rates join the network and offer reduced-fee sessions. Clients pay a one-time $65 lifetime membership fee, then book sessions at $30–80 each. The therapists on Open Path are fully licensed — not interns, not coaches, not unlicensed practitioners. They're choosing to offer reduced rates as part of their practice, and Open Path is the infrastructure that connects them to clients who need it.

This is not the same as a platform charging a subscription and discounting from artificially high list prices. These are real therapists making a genuine clinical commitment to accessible care. If you're uninsured, underinsured, or simply at a financial position where $150–250/session is not viable — Open Path is the most honest answer to that problem that we've found.

Pricing & Plans

Open Path's pricing model is unusual and worth understanding clearly. You pay a one-time $65 lifetime membership fee when you join. After that, you pay your therapist directly for each session at their listed reduced rate — typically $30–80. There are no monthly subscriptions, no platform fees, no percentage taken from sessions. The $65 buys you permanent access to the directory and the ability to book reduced-rate sessions indefinitely. At $40/session, 10 sessions cost you $465 total ($65 membership + $400 in sessions). At standard rates of $175/session, the same 10 sessions would cost $1,750.

Who qualifies:

Open Path is designed for people experiencing financial hardship — specifically those with household incomes that make standard therapy rates genuinely prohibitive. There's a self-attestation process during membership signup. Open Path trusts clients to be honest about their financial situation, and the model depends on that honesty being the norm. It's not audited, but it's also not meant to be gamed by people who could afford standard rates.

The therapists on Open Path made a choice. They decided accessible care was part of what they do — not a charity exception, but a built-in part of their practice. That distinction shows up in the quality of the work.

What We Like

Fully licensed therapists at genuinely reduced rates
Not interns. Not coaches. Licensed psychologists, LCSWs, LMFTs, and LPCs who hold active clinical licenses and are choosing to serve clients at reduced fees. The credential quality on Open Path is equivalent to private practice — because these are private practice therapists.
One-time fee, no expiration
The $65 membership doesn't expire. You're not on a monthly subscription. You take a break from therapy, come back six months later, your membership still works. For people whose financial situation fluctuates, this permanence matters.
Both video and in-person available
Some Open Path therapists offer in-person sessions at their practice location at the same reduced rate. If you're someone who does better in a physical office, this is worth filtering for when browsing the directory.

Where It Falls Short

Availability is limited and inconsistent
The best therapists on Open Path fill their reduced-fee slots quickly. You may browse a profile, like what you read, and find no availability for weeks. This is the nature of the model — therapists set their own limited capacity for reduced-rate clients. Budget time for this when you start.
No platform features — just the directory
Open Path is a matching directory, not a therapy platform. There's no video infrastructure built in, no messaging system, no symptom tracking. You find a therapist, contact them directly, and set up sessions through whatever system they use. Less friction than it sounds, but worth knowing.
Not for people who could use insurance instead
If you have insurance that covers mental health services, Headway or Grow Therapy will connect you to in-network therapists at your copay. Open Path is specifically for people for whom insurance isn't the answer — uninsured, underinsured, or in a coverage gap.

Who Open Path Is Best For

Uninsured people who can't afford standard rates
The platform's entire purpose. If therapy has been inaccessible because of cost and you don't have insurance to offset it, this is the right place to start.
People between jobs, in financial transition, or underinsured
The one-time membership model means you're not locked into a subscription during an already financially stressful time. Pay the $65 once and access the directory as your situation evolves.
People who want a real therapist, not a platform experience
Open Path therapists are independent practitioners. The relationship is between you and your therapist, not mediated by platform features. If that's the kind of therapeutic relationship you want, the model suits it.

If cost is what's been standing between you and therapy, Open Path is the most legitimate, sustainable answer we've found. Licensed therapists, real reduced rates, one fee that never expires.
