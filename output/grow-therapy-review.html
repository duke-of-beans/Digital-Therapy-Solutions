Grow Therapy at a Glance

Grow Therapy

Insurance-covered therapy where you pick your own therapist

4.3 Recommended
Our Verdict
The rare combination of insurance coverage and genuine therapist choice. If you have insurance and want to pick who you see, Grow Therapy is the strongest option in this category.

Most therapy platforms make the matching decision for you. You fill out a questionnaire and an algorithm picks your therapist. Grow Therapy doesn't do that. You get a directory of real therapist profiles — full bios, listed specializations, session availability, accepted insurance plans — and you book the one you want. It sounds simple, but it's genuinely uncommon in online therapy. The combination of insurance acceptance and profile-first browsing is what makes Grow Therapy worth considering seriously. The network has grown fast, covering most major insurance plans and a wide range of specialties. The tradeoffs are real too: no messaging between sessions, no psychiatry, and availability varies by location. But for the specific person this is built for — insured, wants choice, prioritizes fit over speed — it works.

Pricing & Plans

Grow Therapy bills your insurance directly, which means your cost is whatever your plan's in-network mental health copay is — typically $20–50 per session. Grow Therapy works with Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, Optum, and a growing list of regional plans. The best way to confirm coverage is to search by your specific insurance on the Grow Therapy site before browsing therapists.

Self-pay rates exist and are set by individual therapists on the platform — typically $100–200 per session. At that price, Grow Therapy doesn't have a compelling advantage over other platforms. The value proposition here is almost entirely insurance-driven.

How to verify your coverage before you start:

Go to growtherapy.com and enter your insurance plan before searching for therapists. The platform filters results to show only in-network providers for your carrier. This is the right first step — don't browse profiles and fall in love with someone before confirming they take your insurance.

The ability to read a therapist's actual biography and specializations before committing to a first session changes the probability of a good fit — and a good fit is what makes therapy work.

Session Formats

Grow Therapy supports video and in-person sessions. Which formats are available depends on the individual therapist — many offer both, some are video-only, and some practice locations offer in-person for local clients. The session itself is between you and your therapist. Grow Therapy handles the scheduling, insurance billing, and payment; the actual session is a standard clinical appointment.

Video sessions

Conducted through Grow Therapy's integrated video platform. Most therapists on the network offer this. Good for people across the country, not just where in-person therapists are geographically concentrated.

In-person sessions

Available from therapists who operate a physical practice location. Filtering for in-person on Grow Therapy narrows to providers near you. Insurance coverage applies to in-person sessions the same way it does for video.

Grow Therapy does not offer between-session messaging. Sessions are discrete appointments — not a subscription service. If you want async messaging between sessions, BetterHelp offers that as part of its model.

What We Like

You actually choose your therapist
Not an algorithm pick, not a mystery match — a directory of real profiles where you read the bio, check the specialties, confirm the insurance, and book. The transparency of this is underrated. If the first person didn't work, you know exactly who else is available and why.
Insurance billing is handled for you
Grow Therapy bills your insurer directly — no superbills, no out-of-network reimbursement paperwork, no guessing what you owe. You see your copay upfront and pay that. For anyone who's navigated insurance billing manually, this matters.
In-person and video — your call
Many therapists on Grow Therapy offer both formats. If you want to start on video and move to in-person later, or alternate between the two, that's possible depending on your therapist. Flexibility without changing platforms.
Rapidly growing network
Grow Therapy has scaled fast. Specialty coverage, geographic availability, and insurance breadth have all improved significantly over the past two years. If it didn't have what you needed six months ago, worth checking again.

Where It Falls Short

No between-session messaging
Grow Therapy is appointment-based, not subscription-based. You book sessions, attend them, and that's it. There's no messaging layer between appointments. For people who want ongoing contact with their therapist between sessions, BetterHelp or Talkspace offer that.
No psychiatry or medication management
Grow Therapy is therapy only. If you need medication alongside therapy — common for ADHD, some depression, and anxiety cases — you'll need a separate provider for prescriptions. Talkspace and Cerebral both offer integrated psychiatry.
Availability varies by location
Grow Therapy's network is denser in urban markets. Rural and suburban users may find fewer in-network options depending on their zip code and insurance. If availability is thin near you, Headway is worth checking — it operates a similarly large network and the coverage overlap may differ.
Not compelling without insurance
Self-pay rates on Grow Therapy are set by individual therapists and run $100–200/session. At that price you're paying the same as a private practice with none of the extra structure. The value here is insurance. Without it, other platforms offer more for the money.

Who Grow Therapy Is Best For

Insured clients who've had bad algorithm matches
If you've been through BetterHelp's matching process and ended up with three therapists who weren't right, Grow Therapy's profile-first model directly solves that. You control the selection from the start.
People who want a specific specialty or identity match
Grow Therapy profiles include specializations, therapeutic modalities, and identity information therapists choose to share. If finding someone with lived experience relevant to yours matters — it often does — browsing directly is more reliable than trusting an algorithm to get that right.
People who want in-person options alongside video
Grow Therapy is one of the few platforms where in-person and video both exist under the same insurance billing infrastructure. If you want the option to eventually move sessions to an office, this is the place to start.

Who Should Look Elsewhere

People without insurance, paying out of pocket
Without insurance, self-pay rates are $100–200/session with no structural advantage over a standard private practice. BetterHelp ($65–100/week including messaging) or Online-Therapy.com ($45–80/week with CBT tools) offer more value at those price points.
People who want to start immediately
Grow Therapy is appointment-based, which means scheduling around a therapist's actual availability. Wait times vary by provider — some have same-week openings, some don't. If you need to talk to someone in the next 24 hours, BetterHelp's matching speed wins.
People seeking psychiatry, medication, or structured programs
Grow Therapy is therapy only — no prescriptions, no CBT worksheets, no structured programs. For medication management, see Talkspace or Cerebral. For structured CBT, see Online-Therapy.com.

Our Verdict

Grow Therapy earns its place for insured clients who want to make their own therapist decision. The profile browsing is genuine, the insurance coverage is real, and the billing experience is cleaner than most. It's not a fit without insurance, and it won't serve you if you need messaging or psychiatry. But for the right person — insured, selective, values choice — it's a strong option.
