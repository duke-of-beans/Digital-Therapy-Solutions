Platforms We Recommend for Couples

Each platform below was independently evaluated by the DTS Research Team using our 12-point clinical accuracy standard.

Regain

Built specifically for couples — one subscription covers both partners

4.5 Top Rated

Regain is BetterHelp's couples-specific platform, and the focus shows. One subscription covers both partners — no separate accounts, no double billing. You get weekly live sessions plus unlimited messaging between sessions, which is useful for couples who need to process things between appointments. Therapists specialize in relationships: communication, infidelity, premarital counseling, and life transitions. At $60–100/week, it's significantly cheaper than most in-person couples therapists ($150–300/session). The tradeoff: Regain doesn't accept insurance, and you can't choose your initial therapist — you're matched based on a questionnaire.

OurRitual

Structured couples therapy with a clear roadmap — not open-ended venting

4.3 Recommended

OurRitual takes a structured approach to couples therapy — sessions follow a guided program rather than open-ended conversation, which helps couples who feel stuck in the same arguments. Plans start at $208/month on a 6-month commitment, or $310/month for a single month. Each session is designed to build on the last, with exercises between sessions to practice what you've learned. They also offer individual therapy within the same platform if one partner needs separate support. The tradeoff: the structured format may not work for every couple, and the 6-month commitment (while cheaper per month) is a significant upfront investment at $1,248.

Talkspace

Best for insurance coverage — couples therapy alongside individual sessions if needed

4.2 Recommended

Talkspace offers couples therapy as part of their broader platform, which means either partner can add individual therapy without switching providers. Couples therapy runs $436/month for weekly 30-minute live sessions plus unlimited messaging and access to live workshops. Insurance may cover part of the cost — Talkspace works with Aetna, Cigna, BCBS, and others — though couples therapy coverage varies by plan. The tradeoff: at $436/month self-pay, it's the most expensive option here. And the 30-minute session length feels short for couples work, where issues often need more space to unfold.
