Calmerry

Text-first and video therapy at a lower entry price

Our Verdict
A solid, underappreciated option for people who prefer writing over speaking or want affordable ongoing support without a high weekly price tag. Not BetterHelp's scale, but competitive where it counts.

Calmerry sits in the same general category as BetterHelp — online therapy, subscription model, licensed therapists, text-first with video available — but at a lower price point and with a noticeably different emphasis. Where BetterHelp leads with therapist volume and video, Calmerry leads with async text messaging as the primary mode. Every plan includes unlimited messaging with your therapist. Video sessions are available as add-ons or included at higher tiers. The platform is particularly good for people who find it easier to articulate thoughts through writing, who want lower-pressure daily support, or who can't consistently commit to a live video session each week but still want ongoing clinical contact.

The tradeoff is breadth. Calmerry's therapist network is smaller than BetterHelp's, the matching quality is less consistent, and there's less brand accountability behind individual practitioners. But for the right use case — budget-conscious, text-preferred, general anxiety/depression/life challenges — it's a real option.

Pricing & Plans

Calmerry's lowest tier starts around $42/week (billed monthly) and includes unlimited async text messaging with your therapist. Higher tiers add video sessions — one or more per month depending on the plan. The text-only tier is the main price advantage over BetterHelp ($65+/week) and represents a genuine difference in value for people whose primary need is ongoing written communication with a therapist rather than weekly video sessions. Pricing is billed monthly, not on a 4-week cycle like BetterHelp, which is a minor but useful difference for budgeting.

Not everyone can open up on camera with a stranger. Some people think more clearly when they write. A therapy model built around text isn't a lesser version of therapy — it's a different mode that works better for certain people.

What We Like

Lower starting price than most comparable platforms
At $42/week for the text plan, Calmerry undercuts BetterHelp's $65/week entry price for people who don't need video as the primary format. For budget-conscious clients who want ongoing written support, this is a meaningful difference.
Text-first model suits people who write better than they speak
Async messaging lets you take time to articulate what you're feeling before sending it. For people who freeze or rush through things on a call, or who process emotionally through writing, this isn't a downgrade — it's a better fit.
No strict schedule required
Async messaging means you can engage with therapy around a variable schedule — message when something comes up, get a response from your therapist when they're available. For people with unpredictable work hours or childcare demands, this flexibility is practical.

Where It Falls Short

Smaller therapist network than BetterHelp
Calmerry's therapist pool is smaller, which means less specialty depth and more variability in wait times. If you're looking for someone with a specific specialization — trauma, LGBTQ+ affirming care, a specific therapeutic modality — your options are narrower than on BetterHelp or Headway.
Text therapy has real limits for serious conditions
Async text messaging is not a substitute for live clinical contact in moderate to severe cases. If you're managing significant depression, trauma, active crisis risk, or conditions that require real-time clinical judgment, text-primary therapy is not sufficient. BetterHelp, Talkspace, or a platform with in-network live sessions is the more appropriate choice.
No insurance accepted
If you have insurance that covers mental health services, Headway or Grow Therapy will get you to an in-network therapist at your copay. Without the cost advantage of insurance offset, Calmerry's price position is stronger.

Who Calmerry Is Best For

People who express themselves better in writing than in conversation
If video calls feel pressured, or you find you articulate your thoughts more clearly when you write them out, the text-first model isn't a limitation — it's the right format.
Budget-conscious people without insurance who want ongoing support
$42/week is a real price point for consistent ongoing care if you're paying out of pocket. Not cheap, but noticeably less than BetterHelp's floor for comparable access to a licensed therapist.
People dealing with mild to moderate anxiety, depression, or life stress
Calmerry is appropriate for the broad middle of mental health needs — anxiety, depression, stress, relationship challenges, life transitions. For more complex clinical presentations, a live-session platform is a better fit.

Our Verdict

Calmerry doesn't try to be BetterHelp, and that's the right call. It's a lower-cost, text-centered option for people who either prefer writing over video or need to keep their weekly spend down. The therapist network is smaller and the format has real limits for serious conditions — but for mild to moderate needs, with a preference for async communication, it delivers on what it promises.
