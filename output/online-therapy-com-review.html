Pricing & Plans

Online-Therapy.com offers four subscription tiers. Every tier includes the full CBT program, worksheets, journal, yoga videos, and activity planner — the difference is how much live therapist interaction you get.

Basic — $60/week ($240/month):

Unlimited messaging with your therapist. Full CBT program access with 25 worksheets, journal, yoga, and activity planner. Daily therapist worksheet responses Monday–Friday. No live sessions — this is for people who want the program and written support, not face-to-face time.

Standard — $90/week ($360/month):

Premium — $120/week ($480/month):

Everything in Standard, plus a second weekly live session and express (same-day) worksheet responses. Best for people dealing with acute issues who need more intensive support, or those who simply want more live time with their therapist.

Couples — $480/month:

One live session per week plus messaging for both partners. Uses the same CBT-based structure adapted for relationship issues.

The CBT Program — What You Actually Get

This is what makes Online-Therapy.com different. You're not just getting a therapist — you're getting a structured clinical program. Here's what's inside:

8-section CBT course

A structured program that walks you through the core concepts and techniques of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy. Each section builds on the last. You work through it at your own pace, guided by your therapist. This isn't a textbook — it's practical exercises designed to change how you respond to anxious thoughts, depressive patterns, and other challenges.

25 interactive worksheets

Built into the course, not separate downloads. You fill them out in the app, your therapist reviews your responses, and they discuss the patterns they notice in your next session. This collaborative worksheet model is what makes the program feel guided rather than self-help. The worksheets are the bridge between sessions — they keep the work going when your therapist isn't in the room.

Guided journal & activity planner

A digital journal for tracking thoughts, moods, and patterns — the kind of self-monitoring that's central to CBT. The activity planner helps you schedule behavioral activation tasks (things that research shows help with depression and anxiety). These tools work between sessions, keeping you engaged in the process even on days you don't talk to your therapist.

Yoga & meditation videos

Supplementary content focused on the mind-body connection. These aren't a replacement for therapy — they're an add-on for people who find physical practices help manage anxiety and stress alongside the cognitive work. Not every user will use these, and that's fine. They're there if you want them.

Insurance Coverage

Online-Therapy.com does not accept insurance. There is no in-network option, no direct billing, and no way to use your insurance to reduce the cost directly through the platform.

No insurance? The 20% first-month discount on the Basic plan brings your effective first-month cost to around $192 — less than a single in-person therapy session in most cities.

If insurance coverage is important to you — and for most people paying $60–120/week out of pocket, it should be — Talkspace accepts Aetna, BCBS, Cigna, UHC, and more, with copays averaging $15–30 per session. That's a fundamentally different cost structure. See our Aetna, BCBS, Cigna, and UnitedHealthcare guides for details on what's covered.

That said, the 20% first-month discount and the financial aid program for students, veterans, and low-income users do bring the effective cost down. And because the program includes tools (worksheets, journal, activity planner) that you keep working with between sessions, some users feel they get more therapeutic value per dollar compared to conversation-only platforms.

And Online-Therapy.com includes the full CBT program, worksheets, journal, and yoga at every price tier — nothing gated behind premium plans.
Evidence-based approach for anxiety and depression

Online-Therapy.com doesn't just offer therapists who know CBT — it delivers a structured CBT protocol. Specificity matters for outcomes.
Tools that work between sessions
The worksheets, journal, and activity planner are the core of the CBT methodology. You do the work between sessions, your therapist reviews it, and sessions become more productive because they're responding to your actual patterns rather than starting from scratch. Therapeutic benefit every day, not just during scheduled time.

Where It Falls Short

No insurance accepted
Like BetterHelp, Online-Therapy.com is entirely self-pay. At $60–120/week, that's $240–480/month with no insurance offset. If your plan covers Talkspace at $15–30/session, the math isn't close. Insurance coverage should be your first filter when choosing a platform.
Smaller therapist network
Online-Therapy.com has a curated, smaller network compared to BetterHelp's 29,000+. Fewer options if your first match doesn't click, and potentially longer wait times for specific specializations. For niche conditions or specific therapist requirements, the larger platforms offer more selection.
The structured approach isn't for everyone
Some people need open-ended space to talk through what's happening in their life — no worksheets, just a human who listens. Online-Therapy.com's structure can feel constraining. If you want flexibility to take sessions wherever the conversation leads, BetterHelp or Talkspace gives your therapist more room.
May not suit severe or complex conditions
CBT is the gold standard for anxiety and depression, but not the best fit for every condition. Complex trauma, personality disorders, severe eating disorders, and conditions requiring medication management may need approaches beyond what a structured online CBT program can provide.

Who Is Online-Therapy.com Best For?

People with anxiety or depression
CBT's sweet spot — the conditions with the strongest evidence base for this approach. If anxiety or depression is your primary challenge, a structured CBT program is one of the most effective treatments available, and Online-Therapy.com delivers it in a convenient online format.
Self-motivated people who want structure
If you follow through on homework, track progress, and work better with a clear roadmap than an open-ended conversation, this platform was designed for you. The worksheets and program progression give you something concrete to do between sessions.
Budget-conscious users without insurance
At $60/week for the Basic plan (and 20% off the first month), Online-Therapy.com offers the most comprehensive toolkit at the lowest price point among major platforms. If you don't have insurance and need to stretch your budget, this is where to look first. See our affordable therapy guide for more options.
People who want tools that last beyond therapy
CBT techniques are skills you keep. The worksheets teach you to identify cognitive distortions, challenge negative thought patterns, and build healthier responses. Long after your subscription ends, these tools remain. That's the fundamental value proposition of a program-based approach.

Who Should Look Elsewhere?

People with insurance
If your plan covers Talkspace at $15–30/session, the cost difference is substantial. Check our Aetna, BCBS, Cigna, and UnitedHealthcare guides before committing to a self-pay platform.
People who want open-ended talk therapy
If you need a space to process complex emotions, relationship dynamics, or life transitions without a structured framework, Online-Therapy.com's CBT program may feel too rigid. BetterHelp and Talkspace both offer more flexible, conversation-driven therapy.
People who need medication
Online-Therapy.com is therapy only — no psychiatry, no prescriptions. If your treatment plan includes medication (common for ADHD, moderate-severe depression, and some anxiety disorders), Talkspace offers integrated psychiatry alongside therapy.
People with complex trauma or conditions outside CBT's strengths
CBT isn't the best modality for every condition. EMDR for PTSD, DBT for borderline personality, and specialized programs for eating disorders may be more appropriate. If your needs extend beyond anxiety and depression, talk to a provider about the right approach first.
